# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Two groups on shared devices during camp:
- **Campers** (copii/tineri, Romanian-speaking): check their account, individual points, team points, camp game rules, and request items from the camp shop.
- **Staff/counselors**: award points and manage/approve shop requests. Their tooling is secondary to the camper-facing experience for this build but the data model must support it (roles, teams, points).

Primary job to be done: quickly check "where do I stand" (points, team) and "what can I do next" (rules, shop) without friction, often on a shared tablet or phone in bright outdoor light.

## Product Purpose

Betelino is the digital companion for a Christian summer camp. It informs campers about camp games/rules, lets them browse and request items from a points-based camp shop, and gives each camper a personal dashboard showing their name, team, and points standing (individual and team). Success looks like campers checking it multiple times a day without confusion, and staff trusting the data model enough to build point-awarding and shop-approval flows on top of it later.

## Brand Personality

Warm, wholesome, community-first, joyful, trustworthy. Inspired by Betel Dumbrăveni's clean, community-focused aesthetic: welcoming rather than corporate, simple rather than flashy, rooted in belonging (team identity) over competition-for-its-own-sake.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS template look (hero-metric blocks, gradient text, identical icon-card grids).
- Neon blues, pure dark/black themes, cold corporate palettes.
- Anything that reads as a "dashboard product" rather than a summer camp.

## Design Principles

1. **Warmth over slickness** — soft, luminous, natural tones beat polished SaaS gloss.
2. **Clarity for young readers** — short sentences, generous spacing, obvious next actions, large tap targets for shared devices.
3. **Belonging is visual** — team identity (name, color, points) is a first-class, recurring element, not a buried stat.
4. **One page, one job** — each route does one thing well; no single mega-scrolling page.
5. **Trust through simplicity** — account and points data presented plainly and legibly, never buried in nested cards or jargon.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA baseline: solid color contrast, legible Romanian diacritics at all sizes, large tap targets suited to shared tablets/phones used outdoors, reduced-motion support, no reliance on color alone to convey state (e.g. stock/points status).
